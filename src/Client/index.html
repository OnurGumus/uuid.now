Frequently Asked Questions

What is a GUID or UUID?

A GUID (Globally Unique Identifier) or UUID (Universally Unique Identifier) is a 128-bit number used to uniquely identify information in computer systems.

Why do I need Zero GUID?

A Zero GUID refers to a GUID where all fields are zeroed out. It’s sometimes used for default placeholder values or resetting a stored GUID to a blank state.

How does Version 4 (v4) random GUID work?

A v4 GUID is generated with random numbers. It provides a very high chance of uniqueness, making collisions extremely unlikely.

When should I use a Time-Based GUID?

A Time-Based GUID can embed timestamp data. This makes them potentially sortable or trackable by creation time, which is handy for indexing or chronological ordering.

How do I copy a generated GUID to the clipboard?

Simply click the “Copy” button below the GUID display. This will copy the entire 36-character string (with dashes) to your clipboard.
